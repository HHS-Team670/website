Mentors of Homestead Robotics

HRT greatly depends on the expertice and knowledge of our mentors. With their help, Team 670 have evolved to what we are today.

Ronald Crane

Mentor

Team 670 first welcomed Mr. Crane to Homestead Robotics in 2006. Since offering his assistance 9 seasons ago, Mr. Crane has immensely helped with the engineering of the robot. Mr. Crane has degrees in electrical engineering from MIT and Stanford.This year will be his tenth season with Homestead Robotics.


Steven Eaton

Mentor

Mr. Eaton was introduced to the FIRST Program in 2007. Through his years of working as a dedicated mentor, he has contributed extensively to the design and construction of the internal components of our robots. His only wish is that these kind of engineering programs were available to him when he was in high school. Mr. Eaton graduated from CalTech.

Greg Burroughs

Teacher Advisor

Mr. Burroughs first stepped onto the robotics scene in 2012. After graduating with Bachelors and Masters degrees in math from UC Riverside and a Masters degree in computer science from University of Wisconsin, Mr. Burroughs finds that Team 670 is a wonderfully diverse team, comprised of students from various backgrounds and ages-- regardless of experience level, grade or gender, Team 670 wholeheartedly welcomes all who want to join and make a contribution to the team.
